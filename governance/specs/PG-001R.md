# PG-001R — Clean-Room Promotion Gate Specification

**Specification ID:** PG-001R-SPEC-1.0  
**Artifact identity:** PG-001R  
**Implementation class:** Clean-room reimplementation  
**Historical equivalence to PG-001:** NOT ESTABLISHED  
**Evidence ceiling after origin-controlled tests:** R2[self]  
**Authority:** NONE — PRODUCTION PROHIBITED

## 1. Purpose

PG-001R evaluates whether a proposed evidence-level or authority transition may proceed. It is a deterministic, read-only verifier. It does not mutate a ledger, issue a receipt, sign a certificate, close a contradiction, independently verify upstream evidence, or grant operational authority outside its decision object.

PG-001R is a new artifact derived from the seven promotion blockers described in the frozen Lumen Nexus handoff. It is not the missing historical PG-001 source, and its 19-test conformance suite is not the original test suite.

## 2. Input contract

A request contains:

- artifact identity;
- current, verified, and requested evidence levels;
- requested authority level;
- required, verified, and recorded evidence identifiers;
- open contradiction identifiers;
- required and present recovery dependencies;
- self-issued and independent-reproduction receipt facts;
- external-publication usage and receipt binding;
- governance bundle, gate decision, and human authority identifiers;
- clinical scope and clinical safety level.

Inputs are strict. Unknown fields, missing fields, invalid enums, duplicate identifiers, placeholders, negative receipt counts, and evidence-level downgrades are rejected before evaluation.

## 3. Evidence levels

`R0 < R1 < R2 < R3 < R4 < R5 < R6`

PG-001R consumes an upstream `verified_evidence_level`. It does not establish that level independently. A caller that supplies false verified evidence is outside this primitive's trust boundary and must be controlled by receipt, signature, provenance, and replay verification upstream.

## 4. Authority levels and evidence floors

| Authority | Minimum evidence |
|---|---:|
| NONE | R0 |
| RESEARCH | R1 |
| INTERNAL | R2 |
| PILOT | R4 |
| PRODUCTION | R6 |

Any non-NONE authority request also requires a non-placeholder governance bundle identifier, gate decision identifier, and human authority identifier.

These floors are normative for PG-001R v1.0. They are a clean-room policy decision and must not be back-attributed to the missing PG-001 implementation.

## 5. Normative blockers

### B01 — EVIDENCE_CEILING

Block when `requested_evidence_level > verified_evidence_level`.

### B02 — ILLEGAL_SELF_PROMOTION

For any request at R4 or above, block unless:

- the evidence set is not self-issued only; and
- at least two independent reproduction receipts are recorded.

### B03 — OPEN_CONTRADICTIONS

Block when one or more open contradiction identifiers apply to the artifact or transition.

### B04 — MISSING_RECOVERY_DEPENDENCIES

Block when any required recovery dependency is absent from the present dependency set.

### B05 — EXTERNAL_PUBLICATION_INFLATION

Block an evidence increase when an external publication is claimed as evidence without an artifact-bound receipt. A paper, citation, benchmark, or third-party statement is not by itself reproduction evidence.

### B06 — AUTHORITY_WITHOUT_GOVERNANCE

For non-NONE authority, block when any of the following is absent or unsatisfied:

- governance bundle identifier;
- gate decision identifier;
- human authority identifier;
- the evidence floor for the requested authority.

### B07 — CLINICAL_C0_BLOCK

When the artifact is clinical in scope and its safety level is C0, block any evidence increase or non-NONE authority request.

## 6. Evidence invariants

### I01 — REQUIRED_EVIDENCE_SUBSET

`RequiredEvidence ⊆ VerifiedEvidence`

### I02 — VERIFIED_EVIDENCE_SUBSET

`VerifiedEvidence ⊆ RecordedEvidence`

A violation of either invariant blocks the request.

## 7. Decision semantics

The evaluator returns all applicable blockers in deterministic code order.

- If no blocker exists, `allowed = true`, the effective evidence level equals the requested level, and the eligible authority equals the requested authority. This is an eligibility result, not an operational grant.
- If any blocker exists, `allowed = false`, the effective evidence level remains the current level, and eligible authority is `NONE`.

A blocked decision does not mutate the actual artifact state. State transition and receipt issuance belong to separate governed components.

## 8. Fingerprints

PG-001R emits SHA-256 fingerprints over deterministic JSON (`PG001R-JSON-1`) for diagnostics and test comparison.

These fingerprints are explicitly not:

- `triweavon-cjson-v1` receipts;
- Ed25519 signatures;
- witness evidence;
- certificate identifiers;
- proof of historical identity;
- authority grants.

## 9. Conformance suite

The canonical PG-001R v1.0 conformance suite contains exactly 19 named tests. It covers:

- allowed no-op and basic specification promotion;
- B01 through B07;
- I01 and I02;
- positive and negative R4 reproduction conditions;
- positive and negative recovery conditions;
- positive and negative external-publication binding;
- positive and negative authority controls;
- clinical C0 and C1 behavior.

Supplementary property, fuzz, or mutation tests may be added separately but must not be described as part of the canonical 19-test suite.

## 10. Evidence posture

A clean-clone pass of the source-manifest verifier and 19 tests under origin-controlled CI establishes at most R2[self] for PG-001R.

Advancement requires separate evidence:

- R3: a cryptographically valid execution receipt under an approved profile;
- R4: two independent reproduction receipts from distinct witnesses on non-origin hardware;
- R5: an independent implementation from this specification;
- R6: sustained operational evidence and explicit production authorization.

PG-001R does not close C-PR006-001. That contradiction concerns the missing historical PG-001 artifact and must be dispositioned separately.
